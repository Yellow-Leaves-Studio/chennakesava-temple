Sri Lakshmi Chennakesava Swami Devastanam

Simple, modern, traditional, devotional. No photographs of this temple are legally usable yet, so the identity is built from typography, colour, and two forms that belong to this temple in particular — the chakra and the muggu.

Ornament, and where it comes from

A generic mandala would be borrowed. These three are not: the chakra is Vishnu's own emblem and already a radial mandala; the muggu is the dot-grid drawing Telugu households lay at the threshold each morning; the namam is the mark every Vaishnava temple wears. Each is drawn as geometry, held at low opacity, and given room — devotional, but never busy.

The gopuram, redrawn

Redrawn against photographs of the actual tower rather than from proportions. It is far steeper and more slender than a generic gopuram; seven tiers recede above a tall plain gateway, each face packed with rows of niches; and the barrel-vaulted shikhara at the summit flares wider than the tier below it, carrying five kalasas along its ridge. The tiers were not built at once, so they carry the dates rather than decorating with them.

Palette

Markapur is a slate town — it has manufactured slate for generations. That is the neutral: cool grey-green stone, not the warm cream every temple site reaches for. Kumkum is the accent. Gold is reserved for sacred ornament and the sun, and appears nowhere else.

The sun reaches the feet

For about twenty minutes at dawn, light passes through the gopuram and falls at the deity's feet. It is the most particular thing about this temple, and almost nothing has been written about it.

The status badge, in every state

The one component that can do harm. Published hours are the intersection of every source — the earliest closing time anyone reports. So the badge can say "closed" when the temple is in fact open, but never the reverse. Nobody makes a wasted journey because of us. Festival days override the weekly schedule.
