Design proposals · six pages · not yet built

One shape for six different jobs

The built site works and is honest. But every page is the same document: a heading, a Telugu line, some sections, a table. Nothing about the sun page looks different from the directions page, though they do completely different work.

The diagnosis. The restraint is right and I would not undo it. What is missing is that no page has a signature. A visitor who has been to one page has seen the shape of all six, so nothing is memorable and nothing is faster to read than plain prose would be.

Each proposal below gives one page a single element that only that page could have. All are drawn from the vocabulary already in the build: no photographs, no new colours, no libraries, no measurable weight.

Proposal 01 · Darshan

Hours as bands, not numbers

The single most useful fact on the site is buried in a table that has to be read to be understood. Bands are read at a glance, and the Saturday break stops being a footnote and becomes a visible gap.

Now: three rows of text: "Sunday – Friday 06:00 – 20:30", "Saturday, morning", "Saturday, evening". Correct, but you have to parse it.

Gold line marks the current time. Today's row is filled.

Proposal 02 · Festivals

Brahmotsavam as a sequence, because it is one

Nineteen days in a table reads as nineteen unrelated rows. It is actually a procession: a different vahana carries the deity each night, building to Garuda and then the chariot. A rail with nodes says that; a table hides it.

Proposal 03 · The sun

Show the disagreement instead of describing it

This page's real content is that nobody knows the dates. Three sources publish three windows. Drawn as bars, the overlap becomes visible, and the honest answer falls out of the picture rather than out of a paragraph.

Now: a table of three date ranges, then a paragraph explaining that the common overlap is the last week of December.

ttdseva.in
substack account
others
all three agree
10 Dec 23 Dec 3 Jan 20 Jan

The gold band is the only stretch every source covers. That is what the page should lead with.

Proposal 04 · The temple

Let the gopuram carry its own history

The tower is already drawn and already accurate. Annotating it turns a decorative silhouette into the page's argument: this thing was built across four centuries, and you can see the joins.

Now: the gopuram sits at 118px beside two paragraphs, small enough to read as an ornament rather than as evidence.

135 feet, reportedly the second tallest gopuram in the Telugu states, raised across more than four hundred years.

The same drawing, given leader lines and room to breathe. Nothing new is invented and nothing new is loaded.

Leader lines land on the tiers they describe.

Proposal 05 · Getting there

Distance you can feel

A table of kilometres tells a pilgrim in Hyderabad very little. Arcs put the temple at the centre and everything else in relation to it, which is how someone planning a journey actually thinks.

Now: a six-row table where "94 km" and "272 km" look the same size.

Rail and bus stay as text below. The arcs answer "how far is this from me".

Proposal 06 · Home

Answer the question before anything else

Most arrivals want one fact: is it open. Give that the whole first screen, with the tower present and the hours immediately under it, and let the history begin below the fold where it belongs.

If you only take one

What I would build first

In order of how much they earn against what they cost. All six are static SVG and CSS, so none of them changes the page weight in any way you would measure.

  • 01Darshan bands. Biggest utility gain on the site. The Saturday break is currently a sentence people will miss, and missing it means a wasted journey.
  • 03The sun overlap. Turns the page's weakness, that nobody knows the dates, into its most interesting feature.
  • 06Home hero. Cheapest of the six. It is a rearrangement of things already on the page.
  • 02Festival ribbon. Lovely, and it matters most in the one week a year when traffic is fifty times normal.
  • 04Annotated gopuram. Pure craft. No utility gain, but it is the page's only real image and it is currently wasted.
  • 05Distance arcs. Nice, least essential. The table is not actually failing anybody.

Nothing here is in the design document yet. Tell me which of these you want and I will write them up there first, then build.
